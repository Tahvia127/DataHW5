Understanding the Pattern

What Causes Gentrification?

Investors identify "undervalued" low-income neighborhoods (predominantly Black communities). New transit, development projects, and marketing trigger property value spikes. Landlords raise rents. Long-term residents‚Äîwho built these communities‚Äîare priced out and displaced.

Who Gets Displaced?

Black communities are gentrified at 4x higher rates than white communities. Elderly residents on fixed incomes, renters without generational wealth, and families locked into cycles of poverty are forced to leave first, compounding existing inequalities.

What's Lost?

Black-owned businesses, churches, cultural centers, and historical institutions vanish. Social networks fractured. Intergenerational wealth disappears. Not just homes‚Äîentire cultures are systematically erased from the urban landscape.

KEY FINDING: THE CORRELATION

The relationship between home value increases and racial displacement is NOT coincidental‚Äîit's directly correlated. Every 10% increase in property values corresponds with approximately 2-3% decrease in Black population across gentrified tracts.

This is not "organic neighborhood change." It is a systematic extraction process where community land and wealth are transferred from Black families to investors and developers.
